Mice, Yaks, and Spoons!

How do Mice and Yaks use spoons?

Learn More

Mice!

Mice are small rodents that can be found both in the wild and as domesticated pets. Some common mice species are:

  • The House Mouse (Mus musculus)
  • The Deer Mouse (Peromyscus maniculatus)
  • The White-footed Mouse (Peromyscus leucopus)

A mouse drinks around 5.8 mls of water per day, which is about 25% of an adult mouse's body weight.

Yaks!

Yaks are a species of long-haired cattle. There are two types of yaks:

  • The Domesticated Yak (Bos grunniens)
  • The Wild Yak (Bos mutus)

A yak drinks around 9 gallons of water per day, or a whopping 34,068.706 mls!

Spoons!

A United States tablespoon holds approximately 14.8 ml, or 0.50 U.S. fl. oz.

Mice and Spoons!

For a mouse to get an entire day's worth of water intake from spoons, it would need less than one full spoon. 5.8 mls of water would be roughly 40% of a tablespoon's full 14.8 mls capacity.

Yaks and Spoons!

For a yak to get an entire day's worth of water intake from spoons, it would need nearly 2,302 spoons. That's a lot of spoons!
